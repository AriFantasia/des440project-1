THE STORY OF

SUSANO'O-NO-MIKOTO


GOD OF STORMS

Amaterasu, goddess of the
sun, was his sister.

Tsukuyomi, the god the
moon, was his brother.

Susanoo used the “Sword of Length of Ten Fists” as his weapon.

Susanoo and his siblings were born when Izanagi, the god of life, washed his face in a river.

Amaterasu was born when Izanagi washed out his left eye, and Tsukuyomi was born from the right.

Susanoo was born when Izanagi washed his nose.

Susanoo and Amaterasu had a long sibling rivalry. One day, Susanoo challenged Amaterasu. The challenge was who could make the most gods from objects.

Susanoo used his sister’s necklace to create five goddesses,

Amaterasu used Susanoo’s sword to make three gods.

“I’ve won!” Susanoo exclaimed.

“You have not,” countered Amaterasu, “those goddesses were made with my necklace. I have won the challenge.”

Enraged from his loss, Susanoo destroyed his sister’s rice fields, and threw a flayed divine horse through the roof of Amaterasu’s sewing room.

Scared from the sight, Amaterasu hid herself in the heavenly rock cave.

Without Amaterasu, the sun also disappeared.

She left the cave only after much persuasion from other gods.

Susanoo was banished from heaven after what he had done to his sister, and was left to roam the earth.

Eventually, Susanoo came to a village and met a grieving elderly couple who held their beautiful daughter, “What is wrong?” Susanoo asked.

“The dreaded serpent Yamato-no-Orochi, has come to this village each year to eat one of our daughters,” the man explained.

“Soon, Orochi will be back to eat our eighth daughter,” the woman cried.

Moved by their plight, Susanoo told the couple he will kill the serpent if he could marry their daughter. The couple agreed.

“Before the serpent comes, brew your strongest sake. Place eight cups near the doorway of your house and fill it with the sake.”

When Orochi came and smelled the sake, it could not resist and each of its eight heads drank from the cups.

The serpent then fell into a drunken slumber, and Susanoo cut off each of the beast’s heads.

After cutting off the heads, Susanoo discovered a sword in the snake’s tail. The sword was called “Grass Cutter.”

Susanoo presented the sword to his sister as an apology for his misdeeds.
